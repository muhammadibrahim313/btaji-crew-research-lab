Research Session 1: What Research Is & How to Read a Paper Practically (Urdu/Hindi)

Description:
New to research? This first session takes you from zero to the two things every researcher needs: understanding what research actually is, and being able to read a paper without drowning in it.

We cover what makes something research (a clear claim, real evidence, and something new), how to think like a researcher instead of a user, the main types of research, and why research is slow but worth it. Then the main part: how to read a paper, shown live on two famous papers, Attention Is All You Need and ResNet. Along the way we explain the key difference between a limitation and a gap, which is exactly where your own research begins.

Title:
Research Session 2: Gap, Literature Review, Research Question + Practical Project (Urdu/Hindi)

Description:
How do you find your own research? In this session we walk the full path from a simple idea to a clear research question, then show it live on two real projects.

Part A is the method: idea, hypothesis, literature review, analyzing papers (problem, contribution, limitation), finding the gap, and writing the research question. Ibrahim walks through it using his own paper on tokenizer cost across languages. Part B is a full one-hour practical walkthrough, where Zulqarnain takes his final year project on rare cancer diagnosis and shows exactly how he built the literature review, proved every claim with a cited paper, found the gap, and reached his research questions, step by step.
